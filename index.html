How different countries drink their coffee

Sandra Braverman

Whether you're out for brunch with your friends, catching up with an old acquaintance and really testing how far you can go with the small talk, or just trying to survive the day - a cup of coffee always seems to be apart of the equation; for many, their morning ritual cannot be completed without their good ol' cuppa joe. Coffee is enjoyed by many cultures all around the world, and today we will look at how the top 10 most coffee consuming countries like to indulge in this bitter sweet beverage.

After examining that the highest consumptions stems from Europe (in particular in the nothern regions), I decided to do some digging, and really get to the bottom of the most popular orders. With countries like Finland, Sweden, Denmark, taking their coffee with a pastry on the side, we have the oppsoite in Norway, Luxembourg, and Canada, where traditional black or drip coffee is the way to go. Additionally, unlike in Canada - where the drip is enjoyed in the comfort of one's home - all around Europe, it is a social gathering. In Sweden, this moment of gathering is called Fika! - which is a noun to indicate that it's time for a (traditional) black coffee and a baked good.
